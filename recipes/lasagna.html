Lasagna Recipe

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made with layers of pasta sheets, rich sauce, flavorful filling, and melted cheese. Known for its comforting taste and hearty texture, it is one of the most popular homemade meals, perfect for both family dinners and special occasions. The main feature of lasagna is its layered structure, where each layer adds depth and balance to the overall flavor. It is typically baked until the cheese forms a golden crust, then allowed to rest for a few minutes so the layers set properly and the flavors become even more harmonious.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna sheets (pasta)
  • Ground beef (or mixed beef and pork)
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • Tomato paste
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Italian herbs (oregano, basil)
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Ricotta cheese (or béchamel sauce)
  • Milk (if using béchamel)
  • Butter (if using béchamel)
  • Flour (for béchamel sauce)

Here are the basic steps for making lasagna:

  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. Cook the onion and garlic in olive oil until soft.
  3. Add the ground beef and cook until browned.
  4. Add tomato sauce, tomato paste, herbs, salt, and pepper, then simmer the sauce for about 15–20 minutes.
  5. Prepare the béchamel sauce (or ricotta mixture) if using one.
  6. Spread a thin layer of sauce in a baking dish.
  7. Add lasagna sheets, then layer with meat sauce, béchamel (or ricotta), and cheese.
  8. Repeat the layers until the ingredients are used up.
  9. Finish with cheese on top.
  10. Bake for about 35–45 minutes until golden and bubbling.
  11. Let the lasagna rest for 10 minutes before slicing and serving.
